Taiwan

Culinary diversity that makes Taiwan a gourmet heaven.

In Taiwan, cooking techniques from all areas of China have fused, creating a mosaic of both traditional and re-imagined specialties. From gourmet restaurants to night market stalls, Taiwan is sure to offer an unforgetable culinary experience to those with a curious palate.

Stinky
Tofu

You'll smell it long before you see it.

A fermented tofu snack that is deep-fried and covered in sauce. Although its smell is not very convincing, its surprisingly tasty. Look for it at any night market, just trust your nose and you'll spot it.

Xiaolong
Bao

One bite to rule them all.

Chinese Soup Dumplings, or xiaolongbao (小笼包) are steamed dumplings made of very thin wrapper and a filling of pork and hot soup. Yes, the soup is INSIDE the dumpling. Heaven in one bite. Check out this recipe for Xiaolongbao.

Zongzi

A traditional delicacy with a variety as rich as its history.

Zongzi is a traditional Asian rice dish made of glutinous rice stuffed with different fillings and wrapped in bamboo leaves, then steamed or boiled for hours. Read more about some variations of Zongzi one can find in Taiwan.
